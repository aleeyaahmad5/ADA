01

iCare

A calm command-centre for cancer treatment at home — coordinating clinicians, family and the patient's own energy across every chemo cycle. Winner, RMIT × Northern Health iOS Hackathon 2026.

iOS · SwiftUI Hackathon Winner · Team of 4 3-day sprint Prototype
The problem

Going through chemotherapy means juggling appointments, fluctuating energy, and a circle of family and clinicians who rarely see the full picture. The mental load falls on the patient when they have the least to give.

My role

Co-led design and development in a 3-day sprint. I drove the product concept and UX, designed the end-to-end app flow and high-fidelity iOS prototype in Figma, and worked directly with clinicians and mentors to translate real patient needs into the build.

What I designed & built
  • Energy-first home — a daily readout ("Patient Energy: Conserve") driven by overnight vitals, so the patient knows what the day can hold.
  • Care circle & Clinician Hub — oncologist and nurse a tap away, with an iPad-connected real-time vitals dashboard for the medical team.
  • Community task board — meals, transport and check-ins that family and friends can claim, distributing the load.
  • EQ-5D daily check-in — a validated quality-of-life measure (mobility, self-care, activity, pain, anxiety) turned into a 20-second interaction.

CareCircle

One quiet question, answered every week: is my person okay? Passive, contactless monitoring for ageing at home — delivered as a calm SMS, no app required.

The emotional arc — calm reassurance, then a gentle tone-shift: the "aha" moment
The problem

Families caring for an ageing parent live with a low hum of worry — is everything okay over there? Wearables get refused, dashboards go unchecked, and a fall is often discovered far too late.

The approach

CareCircle answers the worry directly. A contactless pad under the mattress learns the patient's own normal, and the carer simply receives a plain-language SMS each week — reassurance when all is well, a soft nudge when it isn't.

The thinking
  • SMS-first, not app-first — meets older carers where they already are; no login, no dashboard, no learning curve.
  • Tone as the product — the shift from "all clear" to "worth a check-in" is the entire value, designed as a felt moment rather than an alert.
  • Two-tap feedback — "All good" / "Needed help" quietly trains the model and proves value with every message.
  • Dignity by design — the best monitoring is the kind a patient barely notices: nothing to wear, nothing to charge.

SaveSmart

A friendly financial co-pilot for the cost-of-living crunch — a multi-agent AI that turns a tight Melbourne budget into clear, doable savings. Built in a 4-day Accenture bootcamp.

Next.js · AWS · AI agents Accenture Bootcamp · Team of 8 4-day sprint Prototype
The problem

Budgeting apps tell you what you spent; they rarely tell you what to do about it. For students and young people facing rising rent and fuel, that gap is the whole problem.

The approach

SaveSmart pairs every expense with a concrete action. A multi-agent AI system (n8n + Claude) generates personalised recommendations, wired to live APIs — Coles, Woolworths, FuelCheck and Eventbrite — so insight always comes with a real next step.

What I contributed
  • Product design & ideation — shaped the experience and wrote the final pitch script delivered to Accenture leaders.
  • Savings-potential meter — translates spending into "you can save $230/month", reframing the budget as opportunity.
  • Live data integrations — Coles, Woolworths, FuelCheck and Eventbrite APIs feeding dynamic, real-time insights.
  • Full-stack MVP — Next.js front end on AWS Lambda + DynamoDB, prototyped and pitched in four days.

UniCube

A playful campus companion that curates RMIT events around students' weekly academic and emotional rhythms. Built in the Apple Foundation Program and pitched to Apple representatives.

iOS · SwiftUI · Xcode Apple Foundation · Team of 6 3-week intensive Prototype
The problem

Universities run dozens of free events — wellbeing sessions, workshops, socials — but they're scattered across emails and noticeboards. Students miss the very things designed to support them.

The approach

UniCube gives campus life a single, joyful home. I led UX and user research — Figma, Apple's HIG and on-campus surveys — and a bold black-and-magenta identity with cube mascots makes a utility feel like something students actually want to open.

What I owned
  • UX design & research — led the research with Figma, Apple's HIG and on-campus student surveys so real needs guided every decision.
  • SwiftUI build — built the core categories & event-detail pages with dynamic date logic for tailored, week-aware recommendations.
  • Brand & identity — the cube mascots, magenta-on-black system and app icon.
  • The pitch — co-created a presentation blending humour and storytelling, commended for authenticity and impact.

The idea

Generative AI is confident even when it's wrong. Food RAG uses Retrieval-Augmented Generation so every answer is grounded in real sources and cites them — accuracy you can trust in the kitchen.

What I built

A RAG pipeline pairing Upstash Vector DB with Groq Llama 3.1 for fast, grounded responses, wrapped in a polished real-time chat — plus an MCP server so external assistants like Claude Desktop can query the knowledge base directly.

Highlights
  • Grounded, cited answers — vector similarity + embeddings deliver hallucination-free responses with sources.
  • Polished UX — multi-session chat history, voice input, dark/light mode and chat export.
  • MCP server endpoint — lets Claude Desktop and VS Code Copilot query the knowledge base as a tool.
